Today I noticed something important from the dentist we visited. My dad pointed out how fast he works, moving from one chair to another at lightning speed. That speed itself was impressive and showed me the value of taking fast action. On the other hand, there was another dentist, a woman, who completely missed the chance to do business with us. She said she would call back when she got free time but never did. Later she called when everything was already done, which was pointless. That showed me how slow decisions and lack of follow-up can make you lose opportunities, while fast movers often win.

I also read Taleb’s Incerto series today. The books are about risk, randomness, and decision-making. The big lesson is that taking risks is essential because the market rewards those who do. Outlier events, which Taleb calls black swans, often define success or failure, so being willing to face them matters.

One example was PewDiePie. He has “skin in the game,” owns his words, and doesn’t back down from controversial opinions. That made him stand out. Andrew Tate was also mentioned in my thoughts as someone who grew by having strong opinions, though his niche is risky and often banned. The insight I took is that to grow a personal brand or a social presence, you need strong opinions and the willpower to stand by them. It works best if your niche is productive rather than destructive.

That was today’s takeaway—move fast, take risks, and own your words.
