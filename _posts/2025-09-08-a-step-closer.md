Today was Monday and I ended up waking at 4:30 in the afternoon. Even though it was late, I worked on my project for about two hours. I tried to build something and I actually did. After that I slept again, then woke up at my usual time. It felt like one step closer to where I want to be, so in my book that counts as a win.

I would not call the day one hundred percent productive. Every time I think I am giving my best, I also feel like maybe it is not truly my full effort. Still, I kept some momentum and that matters.

A special part of today was my dad visiting a Dargah. We are Hindu, but we still go there because of where my parents grew up. Back in their small countryside town, Hindus and Muslims lived side by side. Muslims would even pray outside Hindu temples, and Hindus would also go to Dargahs for blessings. Seeing that, I realize that all the Hindu versus Muslim drama you see in the media is mostly bullshit. In real small towns, humanity is alive. You will find Muslims in temples, Hindus in mosques, and even Sikhs and Hindus in each other’s places of worship.

My dad came back feeling very positive. He felt lighter, with better energy, not lost in the negative thoughts about his condition. That is the power of such places. In our town things can get tense and sometimes communal fights happen, but in villages people happily welcome each other to their Dargahs. And it does not mean you lose connection with your own God. Your God remains yours, and you can still take blessings elsewhere.

I personally believe sabka malik ek hai, meaning there is only one God, whether you call Him Allah, the Hindu gods, the Christian God, or the Sikh’s Waheguru. Maybe that God is just another name for quantum physics, who knows. I should not go too deep into this because these thoughts may not align with the mainstream mindset and could get me cancelled if they spread. But in my heart, I know what I saw and felt.

So overall today was positive. It was not perfect in productivity but still forward moving. And since I woke up at 4:30 today, I believe I can do it again tomorrow, athis time at 4:30 in the morning.
