Today I continued with Taleb’s Incerto, though not through the books themselves but by watching a detailed one-hour YouTube summary. The video had a raw style with a dark background and some transition music that made it engaging. From it, I learned more about fragility, robustness, and antifragility. The key idea is that fragile things break under stress, robust things resist stress, and antifragile things actually grow stronger from stress. Muscles are a clear example of antifragility—when put under stress, they grow.

The phrase that stood out to me was “if it can’t kill me, it will only make me better.” That mindset is powerful and aligns with the idea of embracing stress and risk to grow. I even remembered there’s a song with that same line, and listening to it gives me daily motivation. The bigger lesson is the same one I’ve been learning: risk is necessary, and those who take it are often the ones who grow the most.

On the downside, today was disrupted by long power outages. The electricity went out for about four hours in the morning. I had just started my PC after its usual ten-minute boot-up, and the power went off. I tried again, and the same thing happened. It broke my flow and ruined what could have been a very productive day. I felt I had the momentum to get a lot done, but the outage made it impossible.

Overall, it was a good day for learning but frustrating in terms of productivity.
