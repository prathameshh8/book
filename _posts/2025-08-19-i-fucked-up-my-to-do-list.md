Today wasn’t anything special.

Woke up, made a list, did some bug fixing. My to-do list template got messed up and I lost it. Kind of killed my flow. Unproductive, but still my mistake. I could’ve handled it better.

From Sam Ovens’ course I picked up something: to experience good days you need bad days. Without dark, there’s no light. If everything was just light, you wouldn’t even know what light is. That’s why God throws bad days at us—so we can actually feel the good ones.

I also realized how people treat universities. They were built to make people educated and capable of earning, but now they’ve been turned into some religion. Everyone follows it blindly, thinking it’s the ultimate truth, while ignoring other ways of learning. And the same thing happens in business. People start to be independent but then take VC money and end up slaves to it.

Indie hacking feels like the only clean path. Building independent. Like Telegram. That’s who I want to be. No VC.

- I am greatful for healthy life
- I am greatful for a change
