Today was Saturday and I went to Nagpur. Orange city.

Woke up at 4:30. Had to get pressure out so I don’t end up in Indian public toilets. We took a luxury sleeper bus, but it was no luxury. Bedsheets stinking, not washed. People smelled like rotten curry. Now I know why people say we stink. Some do. Motion sickness was hitting me, but I figured out lying down and covering ears + eyes helped a bit. Still exhausting.

Doctors there told us dad’s caps are misaligned. Basically local doctors are bullshit. Never trust them for major stuff. Big city doctors at least know what they’re doing, but then they tried selling us $2000 worth of caps. That’s like 50% of our yearly income. Fuck no. Not going through that trauma again.

Coming back was hell. It usually takes 2 hours, this driver took 4. Probably high. At least the AC was blasting so I didn’t throw up. But overall, traveling without your own car is just pain. Smelly buses, trash in the seat pockets, uncivilized people. It hit me again—you need your own car. No matter what, I’m getting one. Even if it’s a small Alto.

Nagpur itself blew my mind. Saw Mercedes, BMWs, futuristic cars. Saw metro lines running on flyovers above roads. World-class infrastructure. Never saw anything like this before. Now I get it—big city life hits different. But I still think we need a balance. Small town roots + big city opportunities.

Meanwhile back home, it was Ganpati visarjan. I couldn’t go because of the trip. But my mom went in our neighbor’s Mercedes. He’s a doctor, rich as fuck for our town. People here count even a sedan as luxury, and this guy has a Benz. His wife is close to my mom so she got to ride in it. Funny, my mom sat in a Mercedes before me.

The whole day was exhausting. I was angry, hungry, snapping over small things. But I learned a lot. About travel, money, standards, and how much I want my own car.
