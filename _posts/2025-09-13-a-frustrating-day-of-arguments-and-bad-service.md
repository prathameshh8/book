We planned to go to Amravati early. Dad was like “I don’t want to go,” then changed his mind, but the bus was gone and we were stuck.

Decided to see the local doctor instead. She told us it’s just a gap issue, not catastrophic. Wear a night guard while sleeping. Don’t buy the expensive ones that don’t mold right. She was calm and explained it well.

Dad still didn’t fully believe her. He has this thing where if a doctor is young he thinks they’ll mess you up. Not true every time, but that belief stuck with him.

We had big fights at home. People around my dad—auto drivers, small vendors—carry a victim mindset and it rubbed off on him. Lesson: surround yourself with better people.

Also went to return medicines at the chemist. The guy shouted at us like we ruined his day because the dentist was closed. That pissed me off. He talked like a jerk to customers. We’re never going back to that dentist or chemist.

I was mad as hell. I wanted to confront him, shout back, make him feel ashamed. But mostly I want to cut them out and move on. That’s the day.
