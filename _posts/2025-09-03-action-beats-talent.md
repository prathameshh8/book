Today was a crazy one in terms of mindset.

The biggest thing I learned is simple: take action. Nothing is going to come magically, the universe isn’t just handing out plates of fruit—you have to go out there and grab it. If I’m worrying about something, it just means I’m not taking action. The cure is action. And when I act, that worry transforms into excitement, ambition, and momentum.

Today I was worrying about my content creation and SaaS—whether I’ll have a personal brand, what platform to post on, whether I’ll reveal my face. Too much overthinking. Then I just watched an Iman Gadzhi video about personal branding, and boom—I rediscovered the founder of Lemlist (a guy I was actually searching for days ago). That felt like the universe aligning, but it only happened because I took action. That’s the lesson: the universe gives opportunities once you move.

Most people don’t move. Millions watch motivational videos, but only a few take action. Some take action in the wrong direction. Some iterate, some don’t. The real winners take action, make mistakes, learn, and repeat until the loop compounds into exponential results.

Finding that Lemlist founder was huge for me. He’s built a $150M SaaS and shares how he did it. I realized how important role models are. Humans always needed idols—that’s why gods, legends, and leaders exist. I’m not going to worship him, but I’ll study him. How he behaves, how he builds, how he earns. Same with Iman Gadzhi: at first, beginners admire him, then intermediates reject him, then at a higher level you respect him again. That’s thesis–antithesis–synthesis in action.

Content creation, I now see, is one of the biggest opportunities. Most people don’t know how to do it properly. I’m not saying I’m the best, but I do have experience—hundreds and thousands of views, handling content systems, etc. Even if I don’t monetize directly, I’ll use content to build trust and market my SaaS. That’s the play.

Another insight: don’t waste energy reinventing the wheel. In YouTube/content, innovation happens by small tweaks. Someone tries a new thumbnail style, a new title angle, a new pacing—and it evolves. I don’t need to invent from zero, I just need to copy the packaging and improve by 1–2%. That’s how progress is made.

At the end of the day, trust is the real currency. We live in a low-trust society. Whoever earns trust wins the audience, the market, and eventually financial freedom. People do have money—tons of it. Just because the road outside my house is broken doesn’t mean the whole world is poor. It only means I need to change my location, change my lens. There’s a shit-ton of money out there, and even a tiny slice of it is billions.

Today was ambitious. Today was fire. Today I reminded myself: action beats everything.

- I am greatful for taking actions
